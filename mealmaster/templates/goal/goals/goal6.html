Mediterranean Diet

What is Mediterranean Diet

The Mediterranean Diet is not a fad diet but rather a balanced eating plan inspired by the food habits of people living around the Mediterranean Sea, including Spain, Italy, France, Greece, and Turkey. This diet emphasizes a high intake of fruits and vegetables, along with healthy fats and carbohydrates, primarily through olive oil and spices. It is known for aiding weight loss and promoting overall health, reducing the risk of serious diseases like heart attacks and strokes.



How to Start the Mediterranean Diet

1. Focus on Fruits and Vegetables: Over half of your plate should consist of fresh fruits and vegetables. Salads drizzled with olive oil are common.


2. Emphasize Seafood: Fish is a key protein source, rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which helps with fat metabolism and reduces heart disease risk.


3. Regular Exercise: Incorporate physical activity into your routine.



Dietary Proportions

The Mediterranean diet is structured like a pyramid:


- Carbohydrates (50%): Prioritize whole grains and unrefined carbs from fruits and vegetables.

- Protein (15%): Focus on seafood, poultry, and eggs, limiting red meat to weekly servings.

- Healthy Fats (35%): Use olive oil and nuts daily in moderation.

- Dairy: Consume cheese, milk, and yogurt sparingly.

- Occasional Treats: Enjoy red meat and sweets occasionally.



Recommended Foods and Drinks

1. Vegetables: Tomatoes, leafy greens, carrots, cucumbers, and mushrooms, consumed fresh or saut√©ed in olive oil.

2. Fruits: Apples, bananas, watermelon, oranges, avocados, and pears.

3. Nuts and Seeds: Almonds, pistachios, chickpeas, cashews, sunflower seeds, and chia seeds.

4. Whole Grains: Oats, brown rice, barley, corn, and whole-grain bread and pasta.

5. Seafood: Tuna, salmon, shrimp, squid, and crab.

6. Healthy Fats: Extra virgin olive oil and avocado oil.

7. Other Foods: Fresh milk, yogurt, potatoes, and chicken, along with herbs like garlic and rosemary.

8. Water: Aim for at least 8 glasses daily, drinking some before meals.

9. Red Wine: One glass per day, unless avoiding alcohol is necessary.

10. Tea and Coffee: Consume without added sugar; milk is optional.



Benefits of the Mediterranean Diet

- Reduces overall body fat, including visceral fat.

- Lowers the risk of chronic diseases such as diabetes, heart disease, hypertension, and colorectal cancer.

- Aids gradual weight loss due to lower calorie intake.

- Improves digestive health through high fiber from fruits and vegetables.

- Reduces inflammation and the risk of cognitive decline.

- Promotes healthy, youthful skin due to antioxidants from produce.

- Supports longevity and overall well-being.




Drawbacks of the Mediterranean Diet

- Weight loss may be gradual, not rapid.

- Initial cravings for red meat may occur.

- Not suitable for those allergic to seafood.

- Risk of calcium and certain vitamin deficiencies due to limited dairy intake.

- Ingredients may be moderately to highly priced.
