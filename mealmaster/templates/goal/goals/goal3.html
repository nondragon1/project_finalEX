Paleo Diet

Who is the Paleo Diet Suitable For?

* Those looking to lose weight quickly

* Individuals with digestive issues

* People who are overweight (BMI of 23 or higher)

* Anyone facing health problems related to excess weight



The Paleo Diet mimics the eating habits of our ancestors, dating back to the Paleolithic era. This diet consists of 100% natural foods with no additives. It focuses on proteins, fruits, and vegetables, while excluding bread, processed foods, and dairy products. Following this diet consistently can help with weight loss, reduce fat, and improve body shape. This is why many celebrities and models, who need to maintain their weight and physique, are drawn to the Paleo trend.


The Paleo Diet draws its inspiration from the diet of cave-dwelling humans, who primarily consumed foods provided by nature, such as lean meats, fish, nuts, seeds, vegetables, fruits, and herbs, all in their natural forms. This resembles modern clean eating.


The principle behind the Paleo Diet is to encourage the body to use dietary fat and stored fat for energy instead of carbohydrates, a process known as "ketosis." Importantly, the fats consumed should be healthy ones, making the Paleo approach a highly suitable nutritional strategy for humans.




What Can You Eat on the Paleo Diet?

The Paleo Diet does not have a strict eating schedule but emphasizes clear proportions. It resembles a ketogenic diet in that it involves low carbohydrate intake while focusing on protein, fruits, vegetables, and healthy fats. However, unlike the ketogenic diet, the Paleo Diet allows for a wider variety of foods, including natural sugars from sources like honey or maple syrup.



Here is a breakdown of what to eat on the Paleo Diet:

- Vegetables (40%): Focus on a variety of low-carb vegetables such as leafy greens, carrots, spinach, broccoli, onions, cucumbers, cauliflower, eggplant, and asparagus.

- Protein (30%): Consume lean meats and low-sugar fruits and vegetables.

- Healthy Fats (15%): Include fats from fish, nuts, avocados, olive oil, coconut oil, and fish oil.

- Low-Carb Fruits and Nuts (15%): Choose low-sugar fruits like berries and apples, and nuts such as black beans and white beans.

- Avoid dairy products for at least 30 days.

- Opt for organic ingredients.

- Limit seasonings like salt, sugar, and MSG.

- Avoid processed foods.

- Refrain from alcohol.

- Drink plenty of water (1.5-2 liters per day).


The goal of the Paleo Diet is to return to the dietary patterns of ancient humans by choosing organic, chemical-free foods, avoiding processed items, and minimizing additives to promote health and facilitate weight loss.



Benefits of the Paleo Diet

The Paleo Diet is characterized by low calories and carbohydrates sourced from vegetables rather than wheat. It emphasizes high protein and healthy fats, encouraging the body to burn fat for energy instead of carbohydrates. The vitamins and fiber from fruits and vegetables can also improve digestion. Here are some benefits:


- Effective weight loss

- Strong muscle development

- Enhanced metabolic function

- Reduced fat and lower risk of serious diseases

- Improved digestive health

- Better blood circulation

- Strengthened immune system
